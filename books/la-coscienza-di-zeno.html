The plot of La coscienza di Zeno

At his psychoanalyst's request, Zeno Cosini writes the story of his life: the last cigarette, forever announced and never truly last; his father's death; his marriage to Augusta, the «wrong» sister; his business dealings with his friend and rival Guido.

Zeno presents himself as sick and inept, but his irony overturns every certainty: who is truly healthy in a sick world? A masterpiece of the twentieth-century European novel, set in an unforgettable Trieste.

Why read La coscienza di Zeno to learn Italian

The thematic chapters (smoking, the father, the marriage) read almost like independent stories: you can pause without losing the thread. Zeno's ironic first person shows the passato prossimo and imperfetto in action, with the vocabulary of health, work and family.

About the author: Italo Svevo

Italo Svevo, pen name of Ettore Schmitz (1861–1928), from Trieste, combined Italian and Central European culture. A friend of James Joyce, he was recognised as a great writer only after La coscienza di Zeno, his third novel.

What level of Italian do I need to read La coscienza di Zeno?

This edition of La coscienza di Zeno is rewritten for CEFR level A2. A2 is solid elementary Italian: still simple sentences, but with the imperfetto, the future tense and a wider vocabulary. This reader suits learners with a few months of Italian who want to finish their first complete story.

Is this Italo Svevo's original text?

No, it is an adaptation: the plot and spirit of Italo Svevo's original remain, but vocabulary and syntax are rewritten for level A2, with footnotes, a final glossary and comprehension questions at the end of every chapter.
