The plot of Il paese di Cuccagna

In Naples, Saturday is lottery day, and the whole city — ruined noblemen, clerks, working women, moneylenders — lives in wait for the numbers. The Marquis of Formosa has sacrificed his fortune and his daughter Bianca Maria's health to the dream of the impossible win.

Serao follows dozens of intertwined destinies caught up in gambling fever, in a great choral fresco of Naples: the «land of plenty» promised by the lottery is a mirage that consumes all who believe in it.

Why read Il paese di Cuccagna to learn Italian

A choral novel means many dialogues and many voices: perfect for getting used to the different registers of spoken Italian. Numbers, money and bets provide practical vocabulary, and the narrative connectors are exactly those required at B1.

About the author: Matilde Serao

Matilde Serao (1856–1927), Neapolitan by adoption, was the first woman in Italy to found and run a newspaper. A tireless journalist and powerful storyteller, she portrayed Naples like no one else, between reportage and fiction.

Details and difficulty level
