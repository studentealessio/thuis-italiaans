The plot of Giacinta

Giacinta carries a childhood wound that society will not forgive. As a grown woman she decides to defy convention: she marries a man she does not love and keeps her heart for another, seeking a freedom her time will not allow.

Considered the first verista novel in Italian literature, Giacinta caused a scandal on publication for its clear-eyed, unjudging psychological portrait of a heroine who pays the full price of her choices.

Why read Giacinta to learn Italian

An adult, psychological story for readers who don't want fairy tales, even at A1. The vocabulary of feelings and relationships returns naturally chapter after chapter, settling into memory without effort.

Language focus: what you practise

In this level-A1 edition you mainly practise the present tense, the passato prossimo, basic articles and prepositions, short coordinated sentences. About the author: Luigi Capuana

Luigi Capuana (1839–1915), a Sicilian like his friend Verga, was the theorist of verismo: critic, storyteller and tireless experimenter. He dedicated Giacinta to Émile Zola, master of French naturalism.

Frequently asked questions

What level of Italian do I need to read Giacinta?

This edition of Giacinta is rewritten for CEFR level A1. A1 is the first step of the CEFR: short sentences, present and passato prossimo, high-frequency vocabulary. This reader suits anyone who has just started learning Italian or has completed a first beginner course.

Is this Luigi Capuana's original text?

No, it is an adaptation: the plot and spirit of Luigi Capuana's original remain, but vocabulary and syntax are rewritten for level A1, with footnotes, a final glossary and comprehension questions at the end of every chapter.
