The plot of Il piacere

Rome, splendid and decadent. Count Andrea Sperelli, a refined aesthete, poet and seducer, has made his own life a work of art. But he is torn between two women: Elena Muti, the sensual passion who abandoned him, and Maria Ferres, the spiritual love who might save him.

In the salons, antique auctions and baroque churches of the capital, Andrea pursues pleasure until he loses himself. The manifesto novel of Italian Decadentism, written in prose of dazzling beauty.

Why read Il piacere to learn Italian

The most elegant prose of the Italian nineteenth century, made climbable by footnotes and glossary: the ideal training ground before tackling D'Annunzio's originals. The vocabulary of art and beauty is a treasure for anyone who loves discussing culture in Italian.

Language focus: what you practise

In this level-C1 edition you mainly practise the passato remoto, gerunds and participles, literary vocabulary, shades of register and rhetorical figures. The vocabulary revolves around art, beauty and high society: the key words, with translations, are in the glossary below and recur throughout the text, so you learn them in context.

About the author: Gabriele D'Annunzio

Gabriele D'Annunzio (1863–1938), poet, novelist and legendary figure of Italian life between the nineteenth and twentieth centuries, made the «inimitable life» his banner. Il piacere, written at twenty-five, brought him instant fame.
