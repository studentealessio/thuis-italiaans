The plot of I promessi sposi

Lombardy, 1628. Renzo and Lucia are about to marry, but the bullying don Rodrigo prevents it on a whim, and the timid don Abbondio refuses to celebrate the wedding. So begins a long separation that will carry the betrothed couple through riots, convents and flights.

In the background, Milan in famine and plague, the Unnamed and his conversion, Father Cristoforo and the justice of the powerful. The founding novel of modern Italian literature, here in an accessible version that keeps its greatness intact.

Why read I promessi sposi to learn Italian

This is the novel every Italian read at school: knowing it means sharing a piece of common culture and understanding countless everyday quotations. Indirect speech and complex sentences come in controlled doses, true B2 material.

Language focus: what you practise

In this level-B2 edition you mainly practise the imperfect and past subjunctive, hypothetical clauses of possibility, the passive and indirect speech. The vocabulary revolves around history, religion and society: the key words, with translations, are in the glossary below and recur throughout the text, so you learn them in context.

About the author: Alessandro Manzoni

Alessandro Manzoni (1785–1873) devoted his life to a single great novel, rewriting it for twenty years and modelling its language on living Florentine speech. I promessi sposi shaped modern Italian itself.
