You already know more Italian than you think. Here's how to leverage the overlap — and the traps to avoid.

The huge overlap

English has borrowed enormously from Latin (mostly via French), and Italian IS Latin's direct descendant. Cognates:

  • English -tion → Italian -zione: nation → nazione, station → stazione, education → educazione.
  • English -ty → Italian -tà: liberty → libertà, university → università, city → città.
  • English -ous → Italian -oso: famous → famoso, delicious → delizioso, curious → curioso.
  • English -ic → Italian -ico: musical → musicale, historic → storico, tragic → tragico.

Whole categories of English vocabulary become "free" in Italian.

False friends (traps)

  • Simpatico ≠ sympathetic. Means friendly / nice.
  • Attualmente ≠ actually. Means currently.
  • Educato ≠ educated. Means polite / well-mannered.
  • Sensibile ≠ sensible. Means sensitive.
  • Libreria ≠ library. Means bookshop. (Library = biblioteca.)
  • Fabbrica ≠ fabric. Means factory.
  • Preservativo ≠ preservative. Means condom. (Preservative = conservante.)
  • Ospite = both host AND guest. Context tells you.

What actually makes Italian easier

  • Consistent pronunciation: read a word, you can say it.
  • Verb endings do a lot of work: you can drop subject pronouns.
  • Only 2 genders (m/f), no case system.
  • Word order flexible: emphasis via position, not new grammar.

The hardest bits: gender agreement, subjunctive, prepositions.
