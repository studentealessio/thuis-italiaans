Italian greetings and farewells

Not just 'ciao'. Here's when to use which greeting — and how to avoid sounding rude.

Download on the App Store

The greetings

  • Ciao — Hi / Bye. Informal. NEVER use with strangers, older people, or in shops.
  • Buongiorno — Good day. Used from morning until roughly 5 pm. Formal-friendly.
  • Buon pomeriggio — Good afternoon. Sometimes used but "buongiorno" covers it.
  • Buonasera — Good evening. Used from ~5 pm onwards, and later even means "good night" (as a greeting, not farewell).
  • Salve — Hello. Neutral formality — safe when in doubt.

The farewells

  • Ciao / Ciao ciao — Bye. Informal.
  • Arrivederci — Goodbye. Formal.
  • ArrivederLa — Very formal (to a single person you address with Lei).
  • Buonanotte — Good night. Only when leaving / going to bed.
  • A dopo / A domani / A presto — See you later / tomorrow / soon.

Formal vs informal

Use tu (informal) with people your age, family, friends, children.

Use Lei (formal — the polite third person) with older people, strangers, shopkeepers, bureaucrats, colleagues you don't know yet. If they invite you to say tu — Diamoci del tu — switch happily.
